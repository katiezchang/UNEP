## 1. NDC Tracking Module
- “Increase to 24% of electricity generation based on renewable energy sources in the Cuban electricity matrix by 2030,” avoiding 30.6 million ktCO2eq during 2014–2030. [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- “Increase of energy efficiency and saving… will avoid 700 thousand ktCO2eq in 2014–2030.” [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- “Less carbon intensive ground transportation… will avoid one million ktCO2eq annually.” [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- “Increase of forest coverage… to 33% by 2030,” adding 165,000 ha (2019–2030) and “removing 169.9 million tons of atmospheric CO2.” [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- “Reduction of greenhouse effect gases emissions in the swine industry in Cuba. Treatment of 100 per cent of waste waters… reducing 8 million ktCO2eq annually (2020–2030).” [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- Updated NDC mitigation costs total USD 13.8 billion (USD 7.723 billion electricity, USD 1.479 billion transport, USD 4.251 billion forestry, USD 335 million agriculture) with USD 8.4 billion expected from international sources. [p.16](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 2. Support Needed and Received
- BUR1 identified support needs: “capacity-building efforts with respect to transparency (USD 3.9 million), climate investment projects (USD 45 million) and technical assistance (USD 43.5 million).” [p.16](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Transparency support needs for 2020–2025 include USD 500 k for periodic training, USD 700 k for baselines and mitigation scenarios, USD 1.5 million for MRV of finance, USD 704 k for BUR2/BTR1 preparation and USD 550 k for NC4 (total USD 3.954 million). [p.20](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Average annual public international climate finance 2010–2018 was USD 23 million (USD 208 million cumulative), split USD 102.1 million mitigation, USD 101.9 million adaptation, USD 50.4 million cross-cutting. [p.37](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Cuba’s mitigation finance gap: required USD 863 million annually versus average international inflow USD 16.9 million (2010–2018), leaving “up to 50 times” shortfall. [p.38](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Domestic mitigation finance: declared USD 337 million annually versus USD 155 million invested in 2019 (2.2× gap). [p.38](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 3. Other Baseline Initiatives
- Tarea Vida financing process: CITMA and MEP defined short-term (2018–2020) resource needs with projections toward 2030. [p.15](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Technology Needs Assessment priorities: shallow-well drilling, rice water management, intensive silviculture with adapted genetic material, elevated coastal housing, natural-gas combined-cycle power, biomass gasification, expanded rail freight technology. [p.20](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- National funds (National Fund for Forest Development, National Programme for Soil Improvement and Conservation, planned Landscape Resilience Fund) channel state budget allocations for agroforestry, silvopastoral and soil conservation measures. [p.37](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 4. GHG Inventory Module
- Inventories compiled for 1990, 1994, 1996, 1998; preliminary outcomes for 2000; 2002 inventory underway; biennial frequency judged adequate for national emissions trend representation. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- Inventories implemented under the “Protection of Cuban Environment and Sustainable Development” programme, with CC:TRAIN/GEF/UNDP support for earlier years and Cuban State financing for 1996 onward. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- National GHG Inventory Team structure: Institute of Meteorology-led methodology group; National Statistics Office data group; sector experts estimating emissions. [p.2-3](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- Methodologies: Revised 1996 IPCC Guidelines, Good Practice Guidance (2000), GPG LULUCF (2003), Tier 2/3 methods for key categories (electricity, livestock, waste). [p.3-4](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- Activity data largely from official national statistics; certain categories rely on directly gathered data. [p.4](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- Key sources (1990–1998): CO₂ from liquid fuel combustion, N₂O from agricultural soils, CH₄ from enteric fermentation and solid waste; 2000 inventory adds CH₄ from oil/gas and CO₂ from natural gas. [p.5](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- Uncertainty analyses progressed from qualitative to Tier 1 quantitative and Monte Carlo Tier 2 for 2000–2002. [p.5-6](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- QA/QC reviews via national workshops; centralized archiving exists but constrained by financial limitations. [p.6](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)

## 5. Adaptation & Vulnerability Module
- “The Cuban archipelago is highly vulnerable to global climate change… turning into a determining factor for sustainable development.” [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- Adaptation priorities (linked to Tarea Vida): coastal population management, resilient infrastructure, agricultural safeguarding (diversification, soil enhancement, tolerant varieties), urban nature-based redesign, beach restoration, water efficiency, mangrove/forest protection, coral reef rehabilitation, land-use planning with hazard scenarios, multi-sector programme execution, monitoring and early warning improvements, public awareness and water-conserving agriculture promotion. [p.17-19](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Institute of Physical Planning estimated USD 782.7 million to replace threatened housing stock, excluding supporting infrastructure. [p.18](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 6. Institutional Framework for Climate Action
- National GHG Inventory Team includes the Atmospheric Chemistry and Pollution Center, Climate Center, National Statistics Office, Cubaenergía, Transport R&D Center, Polytechnic Institute “José Antonio Echeverría,” Agriculture Ministry, Forestry Research Institute, and other data providers, coordinated through contracts or cooperation agreements. [p.3](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- ICC-GCF (chaired by Central Bank, secretariat CITMA) with MEP, MINCEX, MFP, MINAG, INRH apprises high-level project approvals, accredited-entity endorsement, reporting, and Council of Ministers submissions. [p.12-13](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- NDA responsibilities: represent government to GCF, issue no-objections, coordinate communications, request progress reports, disseminate information, manage project withdrawals. [p.13](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Technical unit promotes project ideas, aligns proposals with national plans, trains personnel, establishes submission procedures, maintains GCF project registry, prepares documentation. [p.13](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- 170 officials trained on GCF operations and financing opportunities between 2018 and 2020. [p.12](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 7. National Policy Framework
- “The Bases of the Economic and Social National Development Plan up to the year 2030 is the main tool to achieve prosperous development that is economically and socially sustainable, resilient, and less intensive in carbon emissions.” [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- 2016 Guidelines of Economic and Social Policy prioritize climate change issues. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- State Plan “Tarea Vida” adopted in April 2017 as the main instrument to confront climate change. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)

## 8. Climate Transparency in Cuba
- “There is no Measurement, Report and Verification system in place… an objective that is being worked on today… in the context of flexibilities granted to SIDS.” [p.4](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- MRV subsystem planned to track support received and domestic finance; Enhanced Transparency Framework targeted for 2025 contingent on support. [p.13](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Transparency capacity-building needs include MRV of domestic flows, private finance tracking, methodological harmonization, and aggregation across sectors/provinces. [p.40](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- QA/QC relies on national expert workshops; centralized archives limited by financial constraints. [p.6](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)

## 9. Official Reporting to the UNFCCC
- National inventories delivered for 1990, 1994, 1996, 1998; updates for 2000 and 2002 in process. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
- BUR1 climate finance figures compiled from institutional inputs, noting absence of complete MRV system. [p.16](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- National Communications enumerate needs for observation systems, analytical equipment, global system participation and institutional strengthening. [p.16](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Transparency support plan includes preparing BUR2, BTR1, NC4. [p.20](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)

## 10. Key Barriers / Constraints
- Economic, commercial and financial blockade by the United States is cited as the main obstacle to progress on climate action and national development. [p.1](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- Blockade limits access to international financial resources, supplies and technologies, negatively impacting climate programmes. [p.2](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/NDC/2022-06/Cuban%20First%20NDC%20Summary%20%28Updated%20submission%29.pdf)
- Climate finance assessment must consider blockade impacts; Cuba lacks access to multilateral development banks, restricting investments, equipment and technologies. [p.14](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Capacity gaps across governance levels (risk understanding, indicators, funding access); project development and submission processes remain complex and resource-intensive. [p.38-40](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/resource/UNFCCC_NBF_TA_Cuba_final.pdf)
- Inventory archiving/documentation systems require improvement due to financial limitations. [p.6](https://unfccc.int/sites/default/files/cuba_ghg_inventories.pdf)
